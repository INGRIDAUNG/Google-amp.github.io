The life of an asexual person

by Brandon Chia (Writer) |

Mar 03, 2017

Opinions

I have never ever been sexually attracted to anyone.

Ever felt that spark of excitement when you accidentally brushed the hand of the person you like? Or what about the "love at first sight" feeling of butterflies when looking at someone?

Well, I have never had that feeling for anyone. Ever. And this is because I am asexual, a lesser-known sexual orientation.

Today, I am going to respond to some of the questions and common misconceptions people have toward the one per cent who do not feel sexually attracted to anyone.

The most common question: Does asexuality really exist?

Although less commonly seen or experienced than heterosexuality, homosexuality or bisexuality, asexuality has existed for just as long as the other orientations.

Some think that asexuality is an excuse that unappealing people make up to explain why they aren't in a relationship. But it isn't.


TALK TO THE HAND BECAUSE I AM SINGLE AND I DO NOT WANT TO MINGLE. 

I have been asked out by a handful of people before – some girls, some guys - and made out with some of them too. But I rejected them eventually because I wasn't feeling any attraction towards them.

It's not like I didn't try, but I just don't seem to desire intimate bonds with people.

So asexual people don't get into relationships?

Yes, and no.

One of the emotional byproducts of asexuality is that I do not feel the need or desire to be in a relationship with another person.

Some asexuals do want to be in relationships, but are usually not sexual with their partners. Also, they can be open to all types of relationships, be it hetero or non-heterosexual, based on their preference.

So you never had a crush before?

Crushes, no. Infatuation…yes. The strange thing is, I actually get more infatuated with television, movie and book characters than actual humans.

This is probably because made-up characters are perfect the way they are. I would so totally be interested in Katniss Everdeen from The Hunger Games or Magnus Bane from The Mortal Instruments if they were real.

Do asexuals even know what sex is?

Of course! We went to school and we learnt about reproduction and sexual education at a young age. Plus, I was from an all boys' school; I'd had to have been blind to not see any type of explicit material.

Oh and before you ask, I do not masturbate; never tried, and never had the urge to try. *Gasp, a guy who has never masturbated!*

I get that it feels great for a lot of people, but I've never thought to myself: "Hmm...I need to release some of this pent up sexual frustration."

Maybe you are just feeling this way because you have not met the right one.

Funny, that is what my mum says. And maybe she's right; Maybe there is that one perfect someone out there for all of us, including the asexuals.

While I am definitely not ruling a relationship out in future, the fact that I've not had real feelings for anyone in the past 20 years is quite a clear indication of the direction I'm heading in.

It might get lonely at times for us asexuals, but with great friends and family surrounding us, I don't think we are very bothered that we lack a partner.
